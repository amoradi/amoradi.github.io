## XXIX The Forerunners of The Reformation

Prominent Characters
1) Literary men
> And while many of lesser note, and for smaller crimes, suffered bonds, imprisonment, and death, these writers were allowed, not only to escape the vengeance of the Church, but to pursue their own course. Their attractive literary productions gave them such favour generally, that the priests were afraid to molest them. Thus, in the providence of God, the hitherto half-concealed corruption of morals wich prevailed amoung the clergy, monks and every order of the sustem, was brought out into broad day-light; under the veil of popular poems, pleasant tales, and satires, the corrupt state of the whole ecclesiastical system was exposed.

## Chapter 3 - The Importance of Verbs
Latin verbs have inflections to encode information for **5 characteristics**:

### Person
There are 3 persons in Latin:

- 1st (I, we)
- 2nd (you)
- 3rd (he, she, it, they)

### Number
A verb is either singular or plural

### Tense
Tense, in Latin, refers to a *time reference* (when it took place) and a *duration* (**aspect**). Latin has 6 tenses.

**action in progress**
- present [past]
- imperfect [present]
- future [future]

**action completed**
- perfect [past]
- pluperfect [present]
- future perfect [future]

### Mood
Mood refers to the way a speaker *treats* an action. Latin has 3 moods.
- **indicative mood**
 * treats the action as a fact.
 * I am opening the window.
- **subjunctive mood**
 * treats the the action as an idea.
 * I might open the window.
- **imperative mood**
 * treats the action as a command
 * Open the window!

### Voice
Voice refers to a subject's relationship to a verb as performer or receiver or an action. 

- **active voice**
 * The subject performs the action.
- **passive voice**
 * The subject receives the action.
- **middle voice**
 * The subject performs the action is such a way that the doer is affected by the outcome.
